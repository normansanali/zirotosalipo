How Did Dayton Clarke Die? A Former Kam River Fighting Walleye Forward Passed Away

Who Was Dayton Clarke?

Dayton Clarke, born in 2001, was an immensely talented hockey player who rose to prominence during his time with the Kam River Fighting Walleye, a team in the Canadian Junior Hockey League (CJHL). Known for his lightning-fast speed, exceptional skills, and unmatched work ethic, Clarke was a force to be reckoned with on the ice.

Previously, Clarke played forward with the Kam River Fighting Walleye. He played for the University of Oregon Ducks at the time. In the midst of the 2021–22 season, Clarke, who is originally from Victoria, British Columbia, was acquired by the Kam River Fighting Walleye from the Elliot Lake Red Wings.

During the regular season, he played in 14 games for Kam River and helped the team win the Superior International Junior Hockey League championship with 10 points and 10 goals. The previous season, Dayton Clarke played in 11 games for the Oregon Ducks, scoring four goals and dishing out two assists. Clarke also participated in games for Elliot Lake Red Wings and Kamloops Storm.

His dedication and determination earned him a loyal fanbase, who were captivated by his explosive performances and unmatched enthusiasm. Clarke’s passion for the game was contagious, and he was admired not only for his skills but also for his leadership qualities.

How Did Dayton Clarke Die? Cause of Death

A former Kam River Fighting Walleye player, Dayton Clarke who was playing for the Oregon Ducks hockey team tragically passed away on Saturday night, May 13th, 2023. Reportedly, Dayton Clarke died after being hit by a truck in Oregon

Police in Eugene, Oregon, responded to a complaint of a seriously injured person lying in the middle of E. 24th Avenue, between Kincaid and Harris streets, at 3:54 a.m. on Saturday, according to a press statement from the department. When they arrived, they learned the victim had passed away.
