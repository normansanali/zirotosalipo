Who Is RHOC's Dr Jen Armstrong? Wiki, Net Worth, Age, Height, Married

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, September 16, 2024

Real Housewives of Orange County season 16 is upon us. And this time we have two new faces joining the reality show — Noella Bergener, and our star for the article Dr. Jen Armstrong.

Keep reading this Dr Jen Armstrong Wiki to learn all about her.

Dr Jen Armstrong On RHOC

Dr Jen Armstrong got integrated into RHOC after meeting Gina and Emily in 2020. At the time, she performed cosmetic procedures on the ladies, and soon developed a good bond with Heather. However, Dr Jen didn’t exactly start on the right foot with Noella, indicating a potential beef in the future.

This season, Dr Jen was joined by former stars Shannon Beador, Gina Kirschenheiter, Emily Simpson, Heather Dubrow, and fellow RHOC newcomer Noella Bergener.

Her Bravo bio wrote, “She embodies the alpha female persona as she runs her own practice, provides for her family, and helps her mom prepare for a move to the OC.” 

As for her take on the show, Dr Jen shared that she was “forever grateful for the journey,” and went on to thank Bravo for the amazing opportunity. “Well, the news is out, and so are the botox needles. Here to give the #RHOC a facelift for #Season16!,” she posted over IG.

Dr Jen Armstrong Married Life

Dr Jen Armstrong was happily married during her time in RHOC. Her husband Ryne Holliday first appeared on the show’s trailer, and fans were immediately drawn to this man who was seemingly always without a shirt.

The RHOC star even took to her IG to share the trailer on November 13, 2021, captioned, “Who is ready to meet my shirtless hubby?”

“Who needs a shirt when he looks that good,” a fan commented.

Before marrying Ryne, Dr Jen was dating her ex-boyfriend Bruce Elieff, who reportedly sued her for $850 thousand. According to NY Post, Bruce said that his then-girlfriend Jen had loaned money from him where “$160,000 went to tuition at the University of Hawaii, $10,000 in monthly expenses, $160,000 for cancer treatments and $37,000 to freeze her eggs.”

Then he went on to add, they broke off in 2013 after Dr Jen gave birth to twins he did not father.

Well, the twins were actually born to Dr Jen’s relationship with Ryne. 

Fast forward to 2021, the duo has a total of 3 kids and owned a lovely chihuahua. According to Dr Jen, her husband was the best dad ever, as he always took on “most of the parenting duties with a smile.”

The twins turned 9 that year, and one of them Cece already had an IG account @ceceholliday.

How Much Is Dr Jen Armstrong Net Worth?

Dr Jen Armstrong flaunted a net worth of above $2 million by 2021.

Anesthetic medicine physician and surgeon by profession, Dr Jen has performed a lot of plastic surgeries in her life. Her official website mentioned that she “enjoys helping her patients achieve their aesthetic goals through the use of injectables, lasers, and other non-invasive treatments.”

Here’s her job history: 

  • President & CEO of her eponymous dermatology center (from 2018).
  • President & CEO of Advanced Skincare Surgery and Med Center (from 2016).
  • Medical Director of Synergy Med (from July 2017 to 2018).
  • Practicing/Supervising Physician of Urgent Care of Newport Beach (from July 2016).
  • President of OC Brachytherapy, LLC/Photon Finance, LLC (from Nov 2013 to July 2016).
  • President of Pacific Coast Medical Group, LLC (from May 2013 to Dec 2018).
  • Research Associate of Beckman Laser Institute (from Aug 2005 to April 2012).
  • Assistant Professor of Concordia University (from Jan 2007 to 2008).
  • Teacher at Kaplan Test-Prep (from Sep 2002 to May 2003 ).
  •  Lab Technician at Rowland-Blake Research Group (from Sep 2002 – May 2003).

As for her education, she got her bachelor’s degree from Concordia University, and her master’s in Physiology and Biophysics from Georgetown University. Finally, she then completed her doctorate from John A. Burns School of Medicine.

In case you’re curious, an aesthetic medicine physician in US earned around $122 thousand per annum.

Dr Jen is also a philanthropist who founded FHG Foundation in 2006. She has also been involved with World Skin Cancer Foundation, Ke ola pono o na keiki, and Hawaii Homeless Outreach and Medical Education (H.O.M.E.).

Fun Fact: Dr Jen was once sued by a former patient  claiming “medical negligence, battery and failure to obtain informed consent.”
