Dylan Sprayberry - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, In Relation, Facts

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, September 20, 2024

Table of Biography

Dylan Sprayberry is an American actor and musician. Dylan Sprayberry is popular for his roles in movies and TV shows like “Man of Steel”, “Teen Wolf”, “Shuffle”, “Old Dogs”, and “Light as a Feather” among many others.

Early Life and Childhood

Dylan Sprayberry was born on July 7, 1998, and he is currently 25 years old. Likewise, his full name is Dylan Muse Sprayberry. He is originally from Houston, Texas, United States. He went on to attend West University Elementary until 2005.

Talking about his family, he has a sister named Ellery Sprayberry who is also an actress. Likewise, his parents are his mother Dana Sprayberry, and his father Carl Sprayberry. His family moved to Los Angeles, California in the year 2006 to help them pursue their careers. Later on, he attended Brighton Hall in between pursuing his career.

Professional Life

Professionally, Dylan Sprayberry is an actor and musician. He booked his first acting role at the young age of three in a public service announcement for PBS. He then continued working in commercials and print as a child. As an actor, he is popular for his roles in Man of Steel, Teen Wolf, Shuffle, and Old Dogs among others. His most recent work as of 2020 is Lost Girls: Angie’s Story. 

Moreover, he is also famous as the teen actor who played a young Clark Kent in the 2013 film Man of Steel. For this film, he played a young version of Superman alongside Henry Cavill. Moreover, this is the first film in the DC Extended Universe.

He also worked opposite Amy Adams, Michael Shannon, Diane Lane, and Kevin Costner for this movie.  As a television actor, he has also appeared in Common Law, Glee, and Criminal Minds. He also appeared as Liam Dunbar on the MTV series Teen Wolf from 2014 to 2017.

He made his motion picture debut at the young age of nine in the 2007 film My Father. Before this, he appeared in Further, he gained fame for his role in the 2009 film Old Dogs. For this, he worked alongside John Travolta and Robin Williams among other actors. In total, he has appeared in 18 films and 11 TV shows by 2020.

More Details

After relocating to Los Angeles, he started to work on multiple comedy and drama network television shows. This includes early and Tracey Ullman’s State of the Union among several others. He also played supporting roles in feature films such as The Three Gifts opposite Dean Cain and Bedrooms.

He first appeared on Teen Wolf in a recurring role during its fourth season. Later, he was upped to series regular for its fifth season which premiered on 29 June 2015. Likewise, he also appeared at Comic-Con with the Teen Wolf cast before the show concluded in 2017.

He had originally auditioned for the part of Young Derek in season 3. However, he was too young back then. During his time on the show, he garnered a significant fanbase.

After 2015

Then in the year 2016, he appeared in the adventure film Vanished – Left Behind: Next Generation. The film is based on The New York Times best-selling young-adult book Vanished, written by Tim LaHaye and Jerry Jenkins. This film also features a cast from MTV’s Teen Wolf, Nickelodeon’s The Haunted Hathaways, and Lifetime’s Flowers in the Attic.

Similarly, after the conclusion of Teen Wolf, Sprayberry continued his career with a supporting role in the 2018 thriller film Malibu Tapes. He also went on to script-write and record his debut original EP. He plays the guitar, bass, and drums. Also, before appearing on Teen Wolf, he was in a band called The Dead Toms.

Moreover, he is a fan of punk rock and cites his favorite bands as Black Flag, Minor Threat, The Stooges, Neck Deep, The Story So Far, and Blink-182. Moreover, he cites Jack Nicholson, Robert De Niro, Sean Penn, Joaquin Phoenix, Tom Hardy, and Jake Gyllenhaal as his inspirations.
