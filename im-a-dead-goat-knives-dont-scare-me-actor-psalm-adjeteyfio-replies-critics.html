Im A Dead Goat, Knives Dont Scare Me- Actor, Psalm Adjeteyfio Replies Critics

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, August 19, 2024

Veteran actor Psalm Adjeteyfio says he has developed a tough skin towards trolls who have persistently ridiculed and criticized him for begging for financial aid and the drama that followed him when he finally gets help.

Talking about the changes on Joy Prime, the “taxi driver” star said he wasn’t worried about the situation as he knew how serious his situation was. He described himself a dead goat so knives don’t scare him.

Better known as TT, the embattled entertainer recounted how a man identified as Kwesi, who is a housekeeper of the media personality MzGee, called him to ruthlessly discredit him.

The man said he had leftovers from the dog he was feeding and wanted to bring him the meal.

He recalled the incident saying “the man said he had some leftover from his dogs, and whether he should bring me some.”

“I asked him if he was sane and he responded by insulting me, ‘Kwasia’ [fool],” he said.

T.T. retorted; “I don’t have the time to entertain a sheep like you,” after which he hung up the phone.

Despite the backlash, he does not feel embarrassed when some describe him as desperate among other forms of trolling.

“In Ga, we have a saying. I’m a dead goat, knives don’t scare me,” he defiantly stated.
