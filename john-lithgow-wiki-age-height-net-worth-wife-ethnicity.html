In addition to his acting prowess, he is also acclaimed as a writer, musician, singer, and poet. Notably, he gained widespread recognition for his portrayal of Dick Solomon in the NBC comedy “3rd Rock from the Sun.” Additionally, he showcased his talent in the Netflix original historical drama series “The Crown,” where he portrayed Winston Churchill, the first Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.

John Lithgow’s Age & Early Life

John Lithgow was born on October 19, 1945, in Rochester, New York, United States, into a family deeply rooted in theater. John Arthur Lithgow, his full name, reflects his American heritage, derived from mixed origins including Welsh, English, Scots-Irish, Northern Irish, and Scottish. His father, Arthur Washington Lithgow III, an esteemed producer and director, was the former head of the McCarter Theatre in Princeton, New Jersey. His mother, Sarah Jane, also had a background in acting. His upbringing included his brother, Robin Lithgow. John Lithgow pursued his education at Princeton High School in New Jersey, and later graduated from Harvard College in 1967, holding a bachelor’s degree in history and literature. During his time at Harvard, he actively participated in student theater, honing his skills in acting and directing. His involvement in Gilbert and Sullivan’s Utopia Limited and receiving a Fulbright Scholarship to study at the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art were notable milestones in his early life. Post-graduation, he served as the Director of the Arts and Literature Department at WBAI, a Pacifica radio station nestled in New York City.

John Lithgow Height & Weight

At 77 years old, John Lithgow maintains a robust physical stature, towering at a height of 6 feet 3 inches and weighing in at approximately 78 kg. His distinct features include his blue eyes and grey hair.

John Lithgow’s Net Worth

What is John Lithgow’s net worth? As of January 2024, John Lithgow has amassed a substantial net worth of around $45 million through his multifaceted career as an actor, musician, and author. Notably, during the 1996-1997 season, he earned $75,000 per episode for his role in the comedic television series, 3rd Rock from the Sun. His proficient portrayal in The Crown also garnered significant acclaim, making him the highest-paid actor despite having lesser screen time than the main character, Claire Foy.

Career

John Lithgow marked his initial foray into acting as a child through his father’s productions. His feature film debut came in 1972 with “Dealing: Or the Berkrlly-to-Boston Forty-Brick Lost-Bag Blues.” The following year, he graced the Broadway stage for the first time as a British rugby player in “The Changing Room.” This served as a stepping stone to numerous Broadway productions, including “My Fat Friend” (1974), “A Memory of Two Mondays” (1976), “Bedroom Farce” (1979), and “Beyond Therapy” (1982). Additionally, he ventured into directing and choreography, showcasing his versatile talents in Bob Fosse’s autobiographical film, “All That Jazz,” where he also played the character of Lucas Sergeant. Notably, his rendition of Yoda in the National Public Radio adaptations of “The Empire Strikes Back” and “Return of the Jedi” further solidified his reputation. In the early to mid-2000s, he took on the role of J. J. Hunsecker in the Broadway adaptation of the renowned 1957 film, “Sweet Smell of Success,” staged at the Martin Beck Theatre. His captivating performance in “The Life and Death of Peter Sellers” in 2004 added another feather to his illustrious career. John Lithgow’s professional journey spans across around forty television episodes and films. Notably, his recurring role as Dick Solomon in the television series “3rd Rock from the Sun” and appearances in shows such as “How I Met Your Mother,” “Once Upon a Time in Wonderland” (voice), “Drunk History,” and “Twenty Good Years” have etched a lasting impact on the entertainment industry.

John Lithgow Wife & Marriage

Who is John Lithgow’s wife? John Lithgow is married to Mary Yeager, a distinguished history professor at UCLA. Their marital union commenced on December 12, 1981. Notably, Mary is his second spouse, and together, they have been blessed with two children, Nathan and Phoebe. His first wife was Jean Taynton, also an educator. Their marriage, which began on September 10, 1966, culminated in divorce in 1980. From his first marriage, he shares a son, Ian Lithgow, who has also pursued a career in acting and has provided John Lithgow with two grandchildren through his lineage.
