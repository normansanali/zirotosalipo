Kourtney, Khloe Kardashian's Red Carpet Dresses Are NSFW

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, August 13, 2024

Yowza! Although Kim Kardashian never made it to the 2016 Angel Ball in NYC, Kourtney and Khloé Kardashian arrived in grand style on Monday, November 21, rocking two dresses that were borderline NSFW.

The eldest Kardashian sister, 37, turned heads in a strapless black gown with ruffled accents — and two giant, booty-baring mesh panels on each side, teamed with Stuart Weitzman sandals. She finished the ensemble with sleek, straight tresses.

But Khloé, 32, may have turned up the heat even higher! The Strong Looks Better Naked author slipped into a sheer Yousef Aljasmi gown with strategically placed crystals that left very little to the imagination. She teamed the design with silver lace-up sandals, and rocked glam Old Hollywod curls, deeply parted to one side.

The sisters and their mom, Kris Jenner, attended the event, which supports Gabrielle’s Angel Foundation for Cancer Research, in honor of the late Robert Kardashian Sr., who died at age 59 of esophageal cancer in 2003. “When I met Robert Kardashian, I was 17 years old, and he kind of raised me,” Jenner told the audience of the famed attorney, tearing up. “One of the things he used to teach me and our four children was about philanthropy and about giving — and about doing it diligently, constantly, joyfully and generously. He taught me, and them, that no matter what we do in life, he would have been very proud of us. And what you make, you give back. And you do it without saying a word because that’s what the Bible said. And we did.”

It was an emotional night — both in and outside the event. As Us Weekly reported, Kim, 36, was set to attend in what was supposed to be her first red carpet appearance since her scary Paris robbery, but she ultimately did not make it. After she found out that her husband, Kanye West, was hospitalized for what’s being called a “psychiatric emergency,” the Kimoji creator, who shares two children with the rapper, immediately left NYC and headed home to L.A.
