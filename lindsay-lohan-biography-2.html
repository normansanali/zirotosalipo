Lindsay Lohan Biography

BIOGRAPHY
Lindsay Dee Lohan is an American actress, singer and businesswoman, born on July 2, 1986, in New York. Daughter of Dina and Michael Lohan, a dancer and a stockbroker, Lindsay began working as a child model from the age of 3, represented by the Ford Models agency, appearing in advertisements for brands such as Calvin Klein and Abercrombie. This led her to work on television and stand out in the series Another World (1996-1997), where several publications predicted imminent success. The following year, Disney hired her to star in the film Twins (1998), where she played two different roles; Her performance was well received and earned her a Young Artist Award. Shortly afterward she starred, alongside Jamie Lee Curtis, in the film Crazy Friday (2003), where her performance was one of the most applauded elements and for which she won an MTV Movie Award. By 2004, in addition to starting her singing career with the album Speak , Lohan became a box office figure with two film hits: Confessions of a Teenage Drama Queen and Mean Girls , both of which brought the then 17-year-old girl , to the peak of stardom. But his fame began to deteriorate by 2008, the year in which, despite publishing his second album, A Little More Personal and appearing in films such as Just My Luck and Bobby , the media began to expose his turbulent private life and his two arrests for driving under the influence of alcohol. Lidnsay tried to deal with the bad fame aggressively and began taking on more mature roles, but her attempts failed again and again. In recent years, parallel to appearances on series such as Eastbound & Down (2013) and Sick Note (2018), Lohan has produced her own reality show, Lindsay Lohan’s Beach House (2019).
