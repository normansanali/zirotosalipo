Zach Bryan Releases New EP Boys of Faith Featuring Bon Iver and Noah Kahan Celeb Secrets Country

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, September 19, 2024

Happy last day of summer! This week’s New Music Friday playlist is FULL of fun new releases including from our favorite country music artists.

It’s always a good day when Zach Bryan surprises us with new music.

Bryan has dropped a SURPRISE five-song EP, Boys of Faith, just weeks after his self-titled album Zach Bryan reached No. 1. The title-track enlists the talents of singer + songwriter Bon Iver, while Noah Kahan lends his hand on “Sarah’s Place.”

Earlier in the week, the songsmith shared on Instagram, “Locked myself in a studio all week, write myself through a notebook, walked around with people I love in the city then went campin, felt restful and hopeful, thankful for breathing no matter the day, just grateful.”

Aside from this project, more of today’s top artists released brand new singles and albums that we are obsessed with including, Kane Brown’s “I Can Feel It”, Cody Johnson’s “Watching My Old Flame”, Kimberly Perry’s “Fool’s Gold”,  Lady A’s “Love You Back”, The Cadillac Three’s “Double Wide Grave”, Kolby Cooper’s “Hello From The Road”, Caroline Jones’s “Talking To Milo”, Peytan Porter’s “Speaking of Georgia”, Rachel Lipsky’s “Black Magic”, Luke Grimes’ “Burn”, Wyatt Flores’ “West of Tulsa”, Carver Louis’s “Damn Good Time” and Kindred’s “What An Old Song Can Do”.

This week’s New Music Friday brought brand new albums that we LOVE including Charles Wesley Godwin’s Family Ties, Parmalee’s For You 2, Colbie Caillat’s Along The Way, Brent Cobb’s Southern Star, Emily Ann Roberts’ Can’t Hide Country and Taylor Austin Dye’s Out of These Hills.

Carrie Underwood has dropped a deluxe version of her newest album Denim & Rhinestones. The album has six additional songs including the single “Out of That Truck,” “Drunk and Hungover” and “She Don’t Know” – a live tour performance.

Megan Moroney has dropped a deluxe version of her album Lucky featuring new songs “Fix You Too” featuring Kameron Marlowe, “Nothin’ Crazy” with Mackenzie Carpenter and “Reasons To Stay.”
