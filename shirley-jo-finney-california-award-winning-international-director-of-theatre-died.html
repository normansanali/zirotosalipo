Who was Shirley Jo Finney ?

A Life Dedicated to Theatre

Shirley Jo Finney was a distinguished actress and director born on 14 July 1949, in Merced, California, USA. With a career spanning over three decades, she has delivered exceptional performances in various movies and television shows. Her notable works include the drama series Lou Grant (1977), the animated movie Hey Good Lookin’ (1982), and the drama film Nuts (1987).

Finney’s contributions to the world of entertainment have earned her critical acclaim and recognition from her peers. Her work ethic and dedication to her craft serve as an inspiration to aspiring actors and directors alike.

Finney was a force to be reckoned with in the world of theatre, her death on October 12, 2023, marks the end of an era and leaves an irreplaceable void in the global theatre community.

How did Shirley Jo Finney die?

Finney’s cause of death was leukemia, a type of cancer that affects the blood and bone marrow. Her illness was a long one, but those who knew her remember her bravery and resilience throughout.

Remembering Shirley Jo Finney

Shirley Jo Finney was more than just an accomplished actress and director; she was a trailblazer in the world of theatre.

Her work, particularly as the founding artistic director of the Fountain Theatre, will continue to inspire future generations.
