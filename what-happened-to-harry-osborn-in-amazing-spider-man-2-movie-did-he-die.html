What Happened To Harry Osborn In Amazing Spider-Man 2 Movie Did He Die?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, September 26, 2024

Harry Osborn, who was dying of the same goblin cancer as his father, asked his best friend, Peter Parker if he could help him find Spiderman.

Harry Osborn is a fictitious character who appears in American comic books published by Marvel Comics, most notably in the Spider-Man series.

Stan Lee and Steve Ditko developed him, and he originally appeared in The Amazing Spider-Man having been born in December 1965.

Harry is Spider-alter Man’s ego Peter Parker’s closest buddy, as well as Flash Thompson, Mary Jane Watson’s and Gwen Stacy’s ex-boyfriend and Norman Osborn’s son.

Beyond the comic books, the character has featured in a variety of versions, including cartoons and video games.

Did Harry Osborn Die? He Took The Serum In Amazing Spider-Man 2

In The Spectacular Spider-Man, Harry dies, but he is resurrected in The Amazing Spider-Man 2 and took the serum.

In an effort to validate his father’s wrong, Osborn took the Globulin Green or serum, a substance that was believed to improve the physical qualities of anyone who used it.

However, Harry was unaware that this molecular composition came at a high cost, one that lost his sanity.

During a nighttime party with Fisk, he was attacked by a Russian assassin who dressed as a bartender in order to poison Harry to death. His best buddy Peter covertly discovered the bartender when he “accidentally” bumped into him, saving Harry’s life.

Spiderman’s DNA has the power to recover anyone instantaneously, making his blood essential for Harry’s goblin disease.

Harry discovers a secret room after being rejected by Spiderman’s blood. A secret room packed with hidden weaponry, including a green outfit that heals its bearer quickly.

This serum not only gave Norman all of the abilities of the other Spiders, but it also transformed him into a half-spider beast.

However, the Spider-Man authors planned to disclose that the mystery villain Gaunt was Harry Osborn, who was still alive, some years later.
