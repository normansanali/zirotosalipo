Who is Tony Bennett first wife Patricia Beech?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, August 16, 2024

Patricia Beech is the first wife of the falling hero Tony Bennett who was a singer and was named an NEA Jazz Master and a Kennedy Center Honoree and was the founder of the Frank Sinatra School of the Arts in Astoria, Queens, New York. He sold more than 50 million records worldwide.

Who is Tony Bennett’s first wife Patricia Beech?

Patricia Beech who was Tony Bennett’s first wife, was a model. She studies art at Ohio University. After he study, she met her loving husband Bennett, and the two became engaged and later got married. The duo first got sight of each other at Moe’s Main in Cleveland, Ohio.

Beech left her career as a model after her marriage and became a full-time mother. According to Bennett, he felt “taken with her beauty” in his book, titled The Good Life.

“I met and fell in love with a young woman named Patricia Beech in July of 1951,” he wrote, according to AmoMama. “I was singing at Moe’s Main Street in Cleveland, Ohio, and one night she came in with a date. I could see her from the stage — she was sitting ringside — and I was taken with her beauty.”

Beech married at the age of 19, in February 1952. Her marriage to Bennett did not end up well and got separated in 1965. They later divorced in 1971 due to marital strain brought on by Bennett touring.

Beech and Bennett were blessed with two children who are D’Andrea “Danny” Bennett born in 1954 and Daegal “Dae” Bennett also born in 1955.
